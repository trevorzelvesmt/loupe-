# Competitive Landscape: AI-Curated "Productive Doomscrolling" Feeds (as of June 2026)

> Raw research thread 1 of 4. Sources cited inline.

## Executive Summary

The concept sits at the intersection of three converging-but-struggling categories: **AI news aggregators**, **read-later/knowledge feeds**, and **anti-doomscroll learning apps**. The graveyard is large and instructive — Artifact (Instagram founders) and SmartNews both died/collapsed despite huge resources, for the *same structural reason*: aggregating "all the news, summarized and ranked" doesn't create a durable habit or a defensible business. The survivors that monetize well are either **narrow and opinionated** (Ground News on bias, News Minimalist on significance) or **newsletter/audio repackagers** (Meco, Huxe, Morning Brew). The clearest unexploited gap: **nobody has cracked a genuinely multi-source (video + news + social + self-help), AI-summarized, vertical card feed that feels like TikTok but delivers compounding personal value** — the video-summary tools exist but are utilities, not feeds; the feeds exist but are text-news-only.

## A. Established aggregators / "old guard"

- **Feedly + Leo AI** — RSS reader with "Leo" AI assistant (prioritize, dedup, summarize, custom AI Feeds; tracks business events like funding/launches/leadership changes). Alive, healthy, B2B-leaning. Pro+ ~$8.25/mo, Business ~$18/mo. Web/iOS/Android. Survived by pivoting to professional/market-intelligence; but it's a power-user/B2B tool, not a consumer dopamine feed; setup-heavy.
- **SmartNews** — mass-market news aggregator (Japan unicorn). **Collapsed in US** — laid off 40% of US/China staff (2023); downloads fell ~50%. Killed by ad-model fragility (Apple ATT), leadership churn, undifferentiated firehose.

## B. Cautionary tales (dead/failed)

- **Artifact** — "TikTok for news" by Instagram co-founders Systrom & Krieger; AI-personalized feed, summaries, clickbait-rewriting. **Dead (shut Jan 2024).** ~444K downloads, stalled from ~100K to ~12K/mo new installs by Oct 2023. Lessons: (1) market too small — "the market opportunity isn't big enough"; (2) feature creep / identity crisis (drifted into Pinterest links, Twitter posts, places, AI images); (3) no international traction; (4) AI itself is the threat — chatbots/search answer without clicking; (5) "making the tough call earlier is better."

## C. AI-native news apps (current wave)

- **News Minimalist** — AI reads ~30K articles/day, scores each 0–10 by *significance*, surfaces "the 1% worth reading"; 12 languages. Alive indie darling (repeated HN hits). Free + low-cost premium. Strength: anti-firehose (*less* not more). Weakness: tiny team; excludes the fun/personal content that drives daily habit.
- **Particle** — AI news reader by ex-Twitter execs (Sara Beykpour); "Explain Like I'm Five" + "Just the Facts," bias context, audio, **Podcast Clips**. Alive, well-funded (~$15M+). $2.99/mo. iOS/Android/Web. Best-designed "news without doomscroll"; still news-centric; summaries commoditizing.
- **Bulletin** — indie AI news/RSS reader (by Shihab Mehboob); AI de-clickbaiting + Smart Summary. iOS, free + $3.99/mo. Clean, but thin moat.
- **Huxe** — **audio-first** AI app by ex-NotebookLM/Google team; daily briefings from email+calendar, interactive AI-host podcasts, "Live Stations." Launched Sept 2025, ~$4.6M raised. Free (early). Most "ambient/hands-free" take; personal-data integration is a real moat; audio-only limits the visual scroll-feel.
- **CuriousCats** — "9 AI agents read 100K+ sources overnight" → personalized briefing across news, video, X, expert opinions. New/niche, iOS, freemium. Explicitly multi-source — **closest in spirit to the idea**; unproven retention.
- **Yahoo "Your Daily Digest"** — AI-personalized ~6–8 min audio summary (launched Dec 2025). Free. Lesson: incumbents are bolting AI audio digests onto existing distribution.
- **Perplexity Discover** — AI news/knowledge feed inside Perplexity + "Discover Daily" podcast. Free (Pro $20/mo). Huge distribution; but it's a *retention add-on*, not a habit-forming vertical scroll.

## D. Read-later / knowledge feeds

- **Readwise Reader** — power-user "read-everything" inbox (articles, PDFs, EPUB, email, tweets, YouTube transcripts) + Ghostreader AI; highlights sync to Notion/Obsidian. Alive, profitable, beloved. ~$9.99/mo. Deep moat via highlights + spaced-repetition; not a casual feed; pricey.
- **Matter** — newsletter-first reader, great TTS + AI Co-Reader. Free tier; Premium $60/yr. Narrow (newsletters).
- **Refind** — "brain food, delivered daily," curated high-quality links by topic; ~500K users. Free + Premium. Curation > volume; small/slow-growth.
- **Mem** — pivoted from feed/notes into "AI thought partner" note-taking. Raised ~$40M, struggled to find PMF; cautionary tale on AI-knowledge-tool monetization. Not a competitor anymore.

## E. Newsletter aggregators & newsletter-media

- **Meco** — #1 newsletter reader; pulls newsletters out of inbox into a focused feed; AI audio roundups. Free + PRO $34.99/yr. Solves a real pain → clear willingness to pay.
- **TLDR** — daily tech newsletter; grew to 1M+ subs, ~$5M+ revenue in ~2 years. Proves bite-sized-digest demand (as email + sponsorships).
- **Morning Brew** — business-news newsletter empire ($70M+ revenue 2025, 4M+ subs; acquired by Axel Springer Feb 2025). The most successful "productive bite-sized" business is a **newsletter monetized by sponsorship**, not a subscription app.

## F. Anti-doomscroll / "learning feed" apps (closest *form factor*)

- **BeFreed** — AI learning platform; turns long-form into addictive podcast-style micro-lessons by goals/mood; "feed is knowledge." 500K+ learners. $12.99/mo. Explicitly markets "addictive scroll, but it's learning"; content is self-help/book-summary, not real-time events.
- **Deepstash** — TikTok-style feed of bite-sized "stashes" from books/articles/podcasts. Free + Pro. Proven card-feed format; static library, not live/multi-source.
- **Reelly / BrainScroller / Kinnu** — "scroll and learn" niche apps; direct form-factor analogs.
- **Video summarizer tools** (ScreenApp, BibiGPT, NoteGPT, etc.) — paste a YouTube/TikTok/IG URL → timestamped summary. **Crucial insight:** the *tech* to summarize multi-platform video is commoditized — but these are **one-off utilities, not feeds**. Nobody has wrapped this in an addictive, personalized, ranked vertical scroll.

## Lessons & market gap

**Why they struggle:** (1) "all news, summarized" is a feature not a product — recreates the firehose; ~⅔ of US adults report news fatigue. (2) AI summaries are commoditized and cannibalize the click. (3) News alone = small, fatigued, low-willingness-to-pay TAM, ad-model fragile (ATT). (4) Feature creep kills focus. (5) Proven money = niche-paid (Feedly/Readwise, sticky via integrations) OR sponsorship-at-scale (Morning Brew/TLDR). (6) Incumbents (Perplexity, Yahoo) bolt digests onto huge distribution.

**The gap to exploit:** (a) **truly multi-source**, not news-only; (b) optimize for **"productive dopamine" + positive affect** (News Minimalist proves people want *less*; BeFreed/Deepstash prove the addictive learning-scroll retains); (c) build a **personal-value moat** (personalization + saved knowledge that compounds — Readwise/Huxe style); (d) **monetize** via freemium prosumer ($3–5/mo proven ceiling) and/or native sponsored cards; (e) **don't drift** — one crisp value prop ("doomscrolling that makes you smarter").
